Learn about Mount Math Madness

Mount Math Madness is a weekly problem-solving competition at Mount St. Mary’s University that challenges students to think creatively and engage with mathematics in a fun, low-pressure environment.

Learn more about the School of Science, Mathematics & Technology

Purpose

  • Annual math and logic competition at Mount St. Mary’s
  • Encourages problem-solving, creativity, and engagement with mathematics.
  • Designed to be fun, flexible, and low-pressure “do what you can”

Source: Dr. Gerhart and previous years email

Who oversees Mount Math Madness

  • Managed through mmmpow@msmary.edu (Mount Math Madness Problem of the Week)
  • Organized by faculty within the Department of Mathematics and CS
  • All faculty of Mathematics and CS department can give questions

Source: Dr. Gerhart and previous years email

Who participates in Mount Math Madness

  • Open to all Mount students
  • Faculty and staff may also participate for bragging rights
  • Only current students are eligible for prizes
  • Students may join any week

Source: Dr. Gerhart and previous years email

How students join Mount Math Madness

  • Weekly emails are sent out with three problems
  • No Sign up, no commitment participation is week to week
  • Problems are posted weekly
  • Submissions are accepted until the following sunday at 11:59 P.M. (can change)

Source: Dr. Gerhart and previous years email

Competition Structure

  • Runs for multiple weeks (typically four, but sometimes there can be five weeks)
  • Each week includes:
    • 100 point problem
    • 200 point problem
    • 300 point problem

Source: Dr. Gerhart and previous years email

Prizes

  • Weekly winner: $20
  • End of event tournament:
    • 1st place: $100
    • 2nd place: $50
  • Tournament includes top point earners from the 4 week challenge
  • Weekly drawing is a Amazon gift card
  • Every 100 points = 1 entry into the weekly drawing
  • Top point earners enter into a single elimination tournament for larger prizes

Source: Dr. Gerhart and previous years email

Notable Facts

  • Problems are creative and varied, not just traditional math questions
  • The event emphasizes participation in a low stakes way
  • Builds community within the mount universty
  • Encourages logical reasoning, persistence, and fun competition
  • Event was promoted on campus bulletin boards and a now inactive facebook page but currently via emails

Source: Dr. Gerhart and previous years email

What Students Should Know About Mount Math Madness

  • Spring competition
  • You can join any week no penalty for missing earlier ones
  • Problems are designed to be approachable, even for non math majors
  • Great for:
    • Strengthening reasoning skills
    • Can get a spot in the department news letter
    • Winning small prizes

Source: Dr. Gerhart and previous years email
