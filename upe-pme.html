What are these Honor Societies?

The gatherings related to it on the Mount's campus are normally the inductions, but the larger organizations host conferences to promote student research. Upperclassmen who major/minor in each subject are the main participants, with some Alumni being included as well. A student is invited to join UPE once they become a sophomore or higher and maintain a GPA of 3.0 as well as a major/minor GPA of 3.3 and PME when they maintain 3.0 as well as a math GPA of 3.0

Source: Dr. Hook, Michael Cunningham

UPE and PME

UPE stands for Upsilon Pi Epsilon and is a computer science honors society. Upsilon Pi Epsilon is overseen by Professor Weiss. PMI stands for Pi Mu Epsilon. Pi Mu Epsilon is overseen by Dr. Hook and is managed by its officers headed by its President, Michael P. Cunningham, Jr. Pi Mu Epsilon has a journal that is provided to mount inductees for their first year of membership, and then they can opt into paying for a membership.

Source: Dr. Hook, Michael Cunningham

Why should you join?

There are lots of opportunities to explore the pathways that their majors have to offer. Both local chapters also tend to have open leadership opportunities, with the option to apply yourself above the chapter level.

Source: Dr. Hook, Michael Cunningham
