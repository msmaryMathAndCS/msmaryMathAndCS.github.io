Students of color in STEM club offers a place for students of color who have a interest in having thier STEM voice heard!

Learn more about the School of Science, Technology & Computer Science

Purpose of this club?

  • The purpose of SOCS is to create a supportive network for BIPOC (Black, Indigenous, and People of Color) students in STEM fields. The club provides spaces where students can build identity, support one another, and reduce barriers in their academic and professional journeys. Allies who support the mission are also welcome!

Who oversees this club?

  • SOCS is run by an executive board made up of five student leaders who each represent different departments in STEM. The club also has two faculty advisors who help guide the group and approve major decisions

Who participates in the club?

  • SOCS is open to all students at Mount St. Mary’s University, especially those in the School of Science, Technology, and Mathematics. While the focus is on supporting students of color in STEM, everyone who supports the mission is encouraged to participate.

How does a student join the club?

  • Any student can join by attending events and getting involved. To become a voting member, you need to attend at least three events per semester.

What happens at an event related to the club?

  • Events vary! “Take Your SOCS Off” events are safe spaces where BIPOC students and allies can talk about struggles and find community. There are also cultural events like potlucks, professional development workshops, and volunteer activities such as science demos for local kids.

What are some notable facts about the club?

  • SOCS intentionally includes leadership from different STEM departments.
  • The club hosts both community-focused and career-oriented events.
  • SOCS is committed to inclusivity and does not tolerate discrimination of any kind.

What else should students know when considering their participation in the club?

  • SOCS is a space where your voice matters. You’ll find community, mentorship, and resources that help you grow both academically and personally. Whether you’re looking to connect with others, celebrate culture, or strengthen your resume, SOCS is a welcoming and empowering place to do it.
