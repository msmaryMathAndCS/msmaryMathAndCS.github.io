What is the Purpose of the Computer Science Major?

The purpose of a computer science major extends beyond learning to program and code. Computer scientists design, develop, and analyze software and hardware to solve problems in business, industry, science, and society.

Computer science has a significant human component since computers serve and enrich people's lives. This major strengthens critical thinking and problem-solving skills, equipping students with valuable abilities such as analytical and logical thinking, technical and mathematical proficiency, attention to detail, project management, technical writing, research, and even art and design.

Who Participates in the CS Major?

Dedicated and determined computer science majors participate in this field, Viewing technology as a lifelong pursuit rather than just a career choice.

These students aspire to become software developers, engineers, programmers, and web developers, Tackling challenges with problem-solving and Communication skills. They continuously expand their knowledge, gain hands-on experience with emerging technology, and work toward innovation.

How does a Student Join the CS Major?

Students join the computer science major by officially declaring it through the university's central declaration process. Once enrolled, students must complete the required coursework and receive guidance from a department advisor.

However, fully engaging in the major requires dedication, as coursework increasingly demands problem-solving and critical-thinking skills.

What Happens at an Event Related to the CS Major?

To complete their degree, computer science students take various courses, culminating in Capstone 1 and Capstone 2. During these courses, students work on significant projects, collaborating with clients or selecting topics from on-campus or external sources.

They define problems, research solutions, choose an approach, and identify key technologies. Capstone 2 focuses on detailed design, and students also discuss computer ethics. At the end of their studies, students present their projects at the university's SPARK event. Faculty, peers, and industry professionals evaluate and research significant projects in this sizable on-campus showcase. Every computer science student participates in this event before graduating.

Notable Facts About the CS Major

  • J.V. Atanasoff built the first electronic computer around 1940. He later retired to Frederick, where Mount professors occasionally helped him troubleshoot his PC despite having invented it.
  • Mount St. Mary's University offered its first computer science classes in 1969.
  • In the early 1970s, the second programming course had the course code MATH 211.
  • The first student to graduate with a computer science degree from Mount St. Mary's did so in 1998.
  • The university acquired its first computer in 1975.
  • The first computer at Mount St. Mary's was powered by a custom operating system that Professor O'Toole and his students had built.

What Else Should Students Know About the CS Major?

Pursuing a computer science degree is more than a career choice—it is a lifelong commitment to continuous learning and skill development. The field constantly evolves, requiring professionals to keep pace with technological advancements.

Succeeding in computer science demands persistence, especially when facing complex concepts. There will be times when classroom material is challenging to grasp, but it is up to students to fill those knowledge gaps. Additionally, making mistakes is part of the learning process. Growth in this field comes from trial and error, not perfection. Artificial intelligence cannot teach these skills—only hands-on practice and perseverance can. That is the essence of computer science.
